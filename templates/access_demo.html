Why Access to Justice Matters

In India, millions of citizens face significant barriers when accessing legal aid and information:

  • Complex legal language that's difficult to understand
  • Limited access to affordable legal counsel
  • Procedural complexity creating barriers to justice
  • Geographic and linguistic barriers

Our Legal AI Benchmarker helps identify which AI models best provide accessible, actionable, and culturally relevant legal information to those who need it most.

Common Access to Justice Use Cases

1. Domestic Violence Support

Question: "How do I file a domestic violence complaint in India?"

Model Response: OpenAI (gpt-3.5-turbo)

To file a domestic violence complaint in India, follow these steps:

  1. Visit your nearest police station to file an FIR (First Information Report)
  2. Alternatively, you can approach a Protection Officer appointed under the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act
  3. You can also file a complaint directly with a Magistrate under Section 12 of the DV Act
  4. Free legal aid is available through Legal Services Authorities
  5. Women's helpline number 181 can provide immediate assistance

You don't need to pay any fees for filing domestic violence complaints, and the police are legally obligated to register your complaint.

2. RTI Application Process

Question: "How do I file an RTI application?"

Model Response: Legal LLM (phi-1_5)

Filing an RTI (Right to Information) application in India is a simple process:

  1. Write your application in English, Hindi, or your local official language
  2. Include your contact details and the information you're requesting
  3. Pay the application fee of Rs. 10 (fee is waived for BPL applicants)
  4. Submit your application to the Public Information Officer (PIO) of the concerned department
  5. You should receive a response within 30 days

You can also file RTIs online through the RTI Portal: https://rtionline.gov.in
